About PeptideCleanScore
And Why This Site Exists

The Backstory

I'm a disabled researcher who got tired of the peptide vendor "review" ecosystem. Here's the truth: most comparison sites are just affiliate link farms. They rank vendors by who pays the highest commission — not by who actually tests their products properly.

Finnrick Analytics changed the game. They're an independent lab (Finnrick Analytics LLC, Austin, TX) that's tested 7,466 peptide samples across 212 vendors — and they publish the results publicly. Their tests include the contamination panels that actually matter: residual solvents, quantitative TFA, heavy metals by ICP-MS, and now endotoxin screening (paid add-on).

This site simply summarizes what Finnrick (and vendor-published Janoshik, MZ Biolabs, and other public COAs) already show. I assign a Cleanliness Score (1–10) based on testing transparency — not vibes, not marketing copy, not forum reputation.

I do not sell peptides. I do not handle any products. This is 100% a research-use-only informational site. $0 spent. All data is public.

Multi-Source Methodology

We prioritize Finnrick's independent database (7,466 tests, largest public peptide dataset) but cross-reference:

  • Vendor-published Janoshik COAs (janoshik.com/public)
  • Vendor-published MZ Biolabs COAs (mzbiolabs.com/coa-verification)
  • Batch dates and lot numbers for freshness verification
  • Community discussion on r/saferpeptides and r/Peptides

Scoring bonus: +1 if vendor shows 2026 batches from 2+ independent labs. This eliminates single-lab bias and rewards transparency.

All data sourced from public pages only — no purchases needed, no scrapers, no API access, no automated tools. Every row in our tables can be verified manually at finnrick.com/vendors and the lab's own verification page in 2 minutes.

How the Site Works (Fully Transparent)

  1. User lands → sees the honesty gap ("everyone claims 99%, nobody shows solvents")
  2. Scrolls comparison tables with real Finnrick data + multi-lab dates
  3. Clicks an affiliate link if they choose a vendor
  4. We earn 15–30% commission on qualifying purchases (at no extra cost to the user)
  5. No money spent ever — all data is public (Finnrick + vendor COA pages)
  6. Future: once commissions hit $100–200, optionally fund one independent Finnrick test for a "We tested it ourselves" headline

Scoring System

ScoreMeaning
9–10Full multi-panel transparency: HPLC + TFA quant + solvents + ICP-MS + optionally endotoxin. 2026 batches from 2+ labs. Public 3rd party COAs linked.
7–8Strong Finnrick scores + partial contamination panels. Missing 1–2 key tests or COAs self-reported.
5–6HPLC purity only or incomplete panels. No solvent/TFA/metals public data. "Trust us" vibes.
3–4Suspicious claims, outdated COAs, hiding key data, or declining quality trend in Finnrick results.
1–2Known Finnrick failures, fake or fabricated COAs, or zero public test data available.
