How to Read a Real COA
(Without Getting Fooled)

"99% purity" means nothing if your vial is 40% residual solvent. Here's what every Certificate of Analysis should actually show — and the tricks vendors use to hide what matters.

🔍 The 5 Panels That Actually Matter

1. HPLC Purity (%)
Table stakes. Tells you percentage of the main peptide peak. The trap: only measures peptide-related impurities — NOT solvents, metals, or TFA. Necessary but NOT sufficient.
2. Quantitative TFA (% or ppm)
How much trifluoroacetic acid counter-ion is in your peptide. Your "10mg vial" could contain 7mg peptide + 3mg TFA. Always look for TFA ≤1% or ≤10,000 ppm. This is the #1 thing vendors hide.
3. Residual Solvents (GC-MS)
Manufacturing solvents — acetonitrile, DMF, dichloromethane — left behind after synthesis. A 2023 Belgian study found 5 of 12 "high purity" vendors had solvent levels above safety thresholds. Finnrick includes this in their standard panel.
4. Heavy Metals (ICP-MS)
Palladium, platinum, and other synthesis catalysts with strict pharmaceutical limits. Almost no peptide vendor tests for this voluntarily. If a vendor shows ICP-MS results, that's a massive trust signal.
5. Endotoxins (LAL Test)
Bacterial endotoxin contamination (EU/mg). Critical for anything intended to be sterile. Finnrick now offers this as a paid add-on. Very few vendors include it voluntarily.

🚩 Red Flags on a COA

  • No batch/lot number — can't trace the test to your vial
  • No date — could be from 3 years ago
  • "99.X%" with no other panels — the most common scam
  • Self-issued COA with no 3rd party lab name
  • PDF that looks like it was made in Microsoft Word — fake COA template factories are everywhere
  • "TFA removed" claim with no quantitative proof — if they can't show ppm, they didn't test
  • COA top color doesn't match your vial — different color = different batch = this COA isn't yours

✅ Green Flags — Trust Signals

  • 3rd party lab name visible — Janoshik, MZ Biolabs, Finnrick
  • Batch number that matches your vial
  • Full panel: HPLC + TFA quant + solvents + metals
  • Endotoxin test included voluntarily — this is rare and valuable
  • Finnrick has tested this vendor and scores are public
  • Multiple labs have tested the same batch — consensus > single source
  • COA photo shows the actual vial with visible top color — not just a data table

🔵 COA Top Color — Verify Your Vial Matches

Every test batch has a visual identifier — the color of the vial cap (top). Vendors use different colors for different batches. When you receive a vial, check:

Blue top: Does the COA photo show a blue-top vial?
Green top: Match it to the published COA.
Clear/Silver: Common for domestic US vendors.
Red: Rarer. Always verify against COA photo.

⚠ If the COA shows a blue-top vial and yours has a green top — the COA is NOT for your batch. This is the simplest verification anyone can do, and it's the one vendors least expect you to check.
