Verification guide

Read the missing panels before you trust the purity.

A 99% purity number can still leave the important questions unanswered. CleanPep now weights identity, content, TFA, residual solvents, and endotoxin separately so larger paid panels are visible.

1. Identity match
Confirm the report is for the exact product being sold. A clean BPC-157 result does not prove a Retatrutide vial.
2. Content / fill
Check whether the vial contains the claimed amount, not just whether the main peak is clean.
3. TFA
Look for a specific TFA or counter-ion result. If none is public, CleanPep treats that as a missing safety panel.
4. Residual solvents
Solvent panels are expensive and high-signal. Vendors who publish them deserve visible credit.
5. Endotoxin
Endotoxin is not answered by HPLC purity. It needs its own assay and batch-specific report.
6. Traceable source
Open the lab lookup or aggregator page directly. Screenshots are weak evidence unless the report can be verified.
Red flag: A PDF that says 99% purity but has no identity match, independent lookup, batch number, content result, TFA result, residual-solvent panel, or endotoxin result is thin evidence.
